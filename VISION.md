# caalm web — Vision

## Problem

Did you know that Amazon.com is running daily experiments on your buying behavior? Feeding you sponsored products, many from China, that they think you'll purchase, but that you don't really want. Influencing you to feel a certain way (sense of urgency, stressed, alone), so that they can manipulate you into buying more, now. Currently, there are about 30 experiments, 20 trackers, and over a 100 things that they have built in to get every cent out of you. This is why Amazon is the richest corporation in the world, pulling in $150 billion in revenue last year and paid almost no taxes, while customers have increased spending $x.

While this may piss you off —it surely bugs the heck out of me — at the core, Amazon does provide a good service. It connects the products of the world with consumers in a (mostly) transparent marketplace, delivers the stuff on time, and handles returns beautifully.

So there's this big disconnect between how the product looks and feels today and what it should be.

Did you know that Facebook is tracking what you say to your friends and how you interact with your family, selling it to organizations, and then using that data to trick you to take actions Facebook / advertisers want you to take —whether that's to buy a new pair of slippers, vote for a Russian-backed candidates, or support masked terrorist operations. And it's all hidden to make it look like its part of your normal day to day.

Moreover, Facebook has designed its app to make you feel worried about interacting and staying connected, desiring approval from your social network, and feeling bad when you don't actively engage in the way they want you to interact with the humans in your life. This addiction and social manipulation is designed and run by an anti-social engineer who's actively lied before congress.

With that said, at its core, Facebook is the largest network of humans with great potential to connect families, collaborate with others who share similar interests, and feel known when it feels like everyone around you doesn't understand you.

So there's this big disconnect between how the product looks and feels today and what it should be.

Amazon and Facebook are just two obvious examples of where the core concepts are good but the mechanics of the machine are being skewed by corporate greed to the point of making the products harmful, overwhelming, addictive, and not human centered at all. I've seen it with guitar tabs, flight searches, news sites. It's the price we've paid for cheap / free, but we don't have to any longer.

## Vision

An Internet that makes me feel good interacting with the core services. That the companies behind these services design products that are human-centered and help the person achieve their goals.

These sites will be calm and safe.

## Product

A browser extension that, through modifications to a website's HTML and CSS, re-interprets the service's webpage and makes it calm and safe to use.

It's like wearing safety glasses in the woodshop, seatbelt in a car, or a hazmat suit in a nuclear waste site. The services online are incredibly powerful, and can yield good, but are very dangerous, and sometimes toxic (whether that's intentional or not).

The minimum viable product (MVP) is protection on Amazon, Facebook, YouTube, Kayak, and Yahoo. It's on the Chrome browser as an extension.
